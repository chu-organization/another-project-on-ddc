Ang Panublion Museum

"Panublion," a Hiligaynon term meaning guardian of precious things is the official name of The Roxas City Museum. The whitewashed museum building, once a circular water tank, is a good example of intelligent architectural reuse. In 1910, President Pastor Alcazar, third president of the town of Capiz (1908-12) constructed a water tank near the"municipio"to provide water to the Kapisnon during the dry months. Water flowed from the roof of the two-story municipal building to the tank. The municipio was destroyed during World War II, but the water tank which is 11.5 meters in diameter and 6.10 meters in height, with walls 27.5 cm thick, was spared.

After the War, offices were built around the tank completely hiding it from view. Only a water spout was visible.In 1993, the offices were demolished and for the first time in three decades, the water tank was seen. The old water tank looked quite imposing, like a lady, grown serene,strong yet gracious and to demolish it would be like cutting the umbilical cord to the past. However, the water tank had outlived its usefulness as Roxas City had a new water system. The city government and other citizens came up with the idea of converting it into a museum and the Roxas City Museum, Ang Panublion, became a reality. The museum contains memorabilia of prominent personalities of Roxas City like Pres. Manuel Roxas, singer Jovita Fuentes and other famous Capizenios who has contributed in the arts and politics.The museum also house various relogious icons, artifacts and artworks.

CAPIZ MUSEUM PROFILE

The United Capizenos Foundation Inc. (UCFI) is a non-stock, non-profit organization of Capizeno businessmen, professionals, members of academe, former government officials and development practitioners that was established for the purpose of strengthening public-private partnership to support the development agenda of both the Provincen of Capiz and City of Roxas and achieve a shared vision of "ONE CAPIZ".

As development partner of government, the UCFI endeavors to catalyze collaborative activities and initiate networking and linkaging efforts with national government agencies, local and international donors to generate resources for the advancement of provincial, city and municipal programs and projects.

VISION

Ang panublion Museum is the central repository of our Capiz heritage upholding pride and actively engaging in cultural diplomacy on behalf of the province.

MISSION

To increase knowledge and inspire learning about Capiz through historical reference services and exhibitions.

STRATEGIC GOALS

  • To collect, preserve, study, interpret, and exhibit the cultural and natural history of Capiz province from the historic times to the present, in support of the development of the people and province of Capiz.
  • To create a heightened understanding and interest on the life and contributions of noble capizenous and the formative periods of the province in which tthey lived.
  • To explore, protect and promote archaeological and heritage sites in Capiz.
  • To be a place for local artists and creators to express themselves
  • To engage in cultural dialogues and educational programs that build bridges between and amongst towns and sectorsbin the province.
