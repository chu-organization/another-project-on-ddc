Ivisan Museum

"In Addition to Pan-ay's old and historical "Santa Monica Church with it's popular biggest bell in south east asia and church museum, Ang Balay Ni Nanay Lording may prove to be the best" must not miss stop over" while in this historic town. carefully nurtured in the passing of time, Ang Balay Ni Nanay Lording have existed since it was built during the pre-war years in the early 1940's. Originally, a 2=storey wooden house, the place has been renovated a number of times to keep it in shape after a series of distructive typhoons that hit the place in several decades. the ground floor used to be a "Bodega" where sacks of rice, corn, crops and other agricultural products where stored after harvest. The second floor is where the living room, a dining space, a veranda and several rooms are neatly spaced to comfortably accomodate the family of Nanay Lording. The hard wooden walls and flooring has been replaced with concrete and native-looking tiles to complement the old antique's tat sprawled alongside.

But before we got emerged on the house, let us find out who is Nanay Lording and what made her house so particular to be labelled as "One of a Kind." Let us begin by saying that Nanay Lording already Passed away on at the age of 92. A retired public school teacher who became a barangay captain of barangay poblacion, ilawod, pan-ay even at her old age. The was also the place where she held classes as their school were lacking of school rooms. All her 3 kids, a daughter and two sons where practically raised in the very house in an upsight manner where discipline and respect to elders follow closely their God centered lives.
